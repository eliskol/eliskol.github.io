This past Monday and Tuesday, I went back to Pasadena High School to give two lectures on Fourier series!
The goal of my lectures was to prove Parseval's theorem:
> If $f$ is a continuous real-valued function with period $2\pi$, then $\sum_{n=0}^\infty c_n^2 = \int_{-\pi}^\pi [f(x)]^2 dx$, where $c_n$ are the Fourier coefficients of $f$ with respect to the orthonormal functions <span class="math inline">$\\{\frac{1}{\sqrt{2\pi}}, \frac{\cos(x)}{\sqrt{\pi}}, \frac{\sin(x)}{\sqrt{\pi}}, \ldots\\}$</span>.

Then we used this to solve the [Basel problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basel_problem#A_proof_using_Fourier_series).
